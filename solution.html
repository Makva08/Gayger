Solution

  • We made it possible for anyone to monitor the level of radiation on the location of his/her device from any place in the world. To implement that, we created a web server that receives data from devices. Some of the devices are completely autonomous and require no external power source (power will be provided by solar panel). Others are handheld and can be carried in the pocket. Devices are able to receive updates from the server, if software correction is needed.

  • First, we were planning to use NE555 as the heart of our circuit, as it is a pulse generator IC. However, after its first testing and then looking up the internet, we decided to use the MC34063 chip, which is a DC/DC converter that is used to obtain the necessary high voltage from a low battery one. Its main advantage over simple microchip NE555 or similar pulse generators is that it can control the output voltage and adjusts the parameters of capacitors and resistors to make it stable. Then we decided to use power MOSFET which has big drain-source breakdown voltage and works on high frequencies. It is getting pulses and working as a switch. Then, we decided to use a coil to feed our circuit with current. Next step was to choose timing capacitor, which also controls frequency and stabilizes voltage on the output. We have chosen those parameters by test method on the circuit you can observe on the picture below. Next, we decided to use diode and pnp transistor to defend our IC from the charges, collected on the gate of MOSFET. For diode, as we were working on high frequency, we used schottky diode. Lastly, we used voltage divider to provide negative feedback for IC and we used capacitor to stabilize voltage on tube.
